Testing

Overview

Testing with each participant is required. After creation, your competition is in Test Mode by default.
This allows you to test cameras and judges.
Test mode must be disabled before the start of the competition. Disabling test mode resets the meet to the start and clears out routines and scores.

Camera Testing

Camera requirements:
  • Dedicated STABLE wired internet connection
  • 30 Mbps+ upload speed PER camera at location (speedtest.net)
  • Camera devices:
    • IPhone with IOS 13+
    • Android phone with Android 9+
    • Windows 8.1+ computer with webcam capable of 30fps at 720p resolution (Logitech C920 HD Pro)
    • Camcorder that can connect to computer via usb as a webcam
  • Camera and microphone access allowed in device settings
  • Ability to run zoom video call with no video glitches or stutters
Each camera must:
  • Stream for 5+ minutes
  • Have rapid movement in front of the camera (an actual performance is best)
  • Be observed by the administrator (you)
What to check for:
  • Blurry movement. This is most likely from a low quality camera or computer
    • Find a better camera or adjusting your camera's settings (EG disable auto white-balance and lighting compensation)
  • Glitches, freezing or pixelization. This is a connection issue
    • Ask multiple participants if they all see the same problem to make sure it is on the camera's end
    • Make sure the camera uses an ethernet cable
    • Have the camera run a speed test at speedtest.net and make sure it meets the minimum requirements of 25Mbps upload per camera at the location
      • For example, if you will be running 2 cameras, you need to se 50Mbps upload speed
  • Other issues
    • Some camera may have a hard time connecting. If so refresh the page and hit the start button again.
    • Upgrade your camera setup if nothing else works.

Judge Testing

This will let the judges practice with the live gym system.
Each judge needs to:
  • Login to their appropriate role(s)
  • Run through the judging process at least twice for each role
  • Observe a stream for at least 5 minutes and watch for glitches, freezing, pixelization or lagging behind the other viewers
    • If these are not experienced by everyone it means they are specific to the judges system/internet.
    • Make sure they are connected to their internet via an ethernet cable.
    • If they are unable to reliably view the stream, they will be unable to judge and you may need to find a replacement.
