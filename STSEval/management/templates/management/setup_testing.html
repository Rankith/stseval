Testing

Overview

Testing with each new participant is required. After creation, your competition is in Test Mode by default
This allows you to test cameras and judges, it must be disabled before the start of the competition.
Disabling test mode resets the meet to the start and clears out routines and scores.
Test mode must be disabled before the competition begins.

Camera Testing

Each camera needs to:
  • Stream for 5+ minutes
  • Have rapid movement in front of the camera (an actual performance would be best)
  • Be observed by the administrator (you)
What to check for:
  • Blurry movement
    • This is most likely from a low quality camera or computer
    • Try finding a better camera or adjusting your camera's settings (disable auto white-balance and lighting compensation)
  • Glitches, freezing or pixelization
    • This is a connection issue
    • Ask multiple participants if they all see it to make sure it is on the camera's end
    • Make sure the camera is using an ethernet cable
    • Have the camera run a speed test at speedtest.net and make sure they meet the minimum requirements of 25Mbps upload per camera at the location
  • Other issues
    • Some devices may have a hard time connecting. Refresh the page and hit the start button again if this occurs.
    • Try another device if nothing else works.

Judge Testing

You should use this opportunity to train the judges on how to use the system.
Each judge needs to:
  • Login to their appropriate role(s)
  • Run through the judging process as if the competition was proceeding as normal at least twice for each role
  • Observe a stream for at least 5 minutes and watch for glitches, freezing, pixelization or lagging behind the other viewers
    • If these are not experienced by everyone it means they are specific to their system/internet.
    • Make sure they are connected to their internet via an ethernet cable.
    • If they are unable to reliably view the stream, they will be unable to judge and you may need a replacement.
